#### Lyon
Well, where do I start...?  
It's been a hell of a year, as my term as gamer and resident quizmaster comes to an end... yeah no I don't have the energy (nor ability really) to write a grandiose speech for this, I've already self-certed my all my deadlines into the sun, yet they're still coming after me. I still have things to say though, so here we go:

Thank you to everyone who's helped with FNG & LAN setups, packups, snack runs and all the other 'behind the scenes' stuff, collectively crashing out at the state of the labs at 5am is certainly one way to weather some of the inevitable insanity, as is running off with the good Costco waffles. Gamers my gamers, we've made it through :)

Thank you to everyone who attended one of my quizzes and didn't think it was completely terrible, and my deepest apologies if you *only* attended the BFSL part 2 quiz, that was **not** my finest hour at all. If you ever guessed one of my more niche game picks or my *very specific* song choices correctly, you have my utmost respect.

And finally, thanks for the memories, cause even if they weren't *all* great, I'm still glad I had the chances to do all this crazy stuff.

And with that, I hang up my hoodie, walk off into the sunset, and crumble into dust as I retire (not before hosting *one last quiz* though! you will pry the title of quizmaster from my cold, dead hands until I finish hosting that quiz. after that... who knows?).

So long and goodnight,  
Lyon

#### Laina
hi guys, laina, one of the gaming freps (and incoming gaming coord) here. its somewhat emotional to see the exec handover happen to me. my first interaction with uwcs properly was while i was still in year 13, i was good friends with luna from sixth form and she (and thus a few other people) invited me to come to the term 3 LAN, which happened to start on the day of my final A-Levels, my extra pure paper. i think in some way the fact that that paper was my last is kind of poetic, since its a lot more like uni maths than the rest of the exams lol. i got invited only a few days before LAN so i had to make some last minute decisions, and the entire night before i was planning what i would do. i ended up walking into my exam room with a bag full of clothes and a sleeping bag. finished my exam, and walked down to the train station with 2 of my friends from sixth-form, that would be the last time i saw one of them, and i saw the other one only 2 more times. but i remember that day vividly, taking a train from exeter to reading, getting stranded in reading becuase of the tracks overheating in southampton, then a train from reading to birmingham, then a train from birmingham to coventry. it was a weird experience, it took several hours but i didnt know what to expect, but i think that day and the 2 that followed definitely shaped my experience of what would come of this uni since from that day i ended up getting introduced to all the people which would be the exec that are leaving now. if it werent for those few days, i doubt i would be interacting with uwcs. after all i dont really like programming or any aspect of non-theoretical computer science lol. but at the time, that weekend was arguably one of the most fun weekends ive had in my life, and it was absolutely worth all the energy it took to actually get there. so yeah, my experience with this university is shaped by uwcs, rather than the other way round. so seeing the exec handover, i feel emotional. its a pretty big turning point for me to know that i get to have the honours of having the role handed over to me, and i doubt id want my university experience any other way. FNGs are great and i think theyre something greater than just gaming, since so many friend groups, unofficial other-society hangouts, actual chances to do organised gaming events, etc. its quite special in my mind even if its a weekly thing. but also, LAN of course, for many the reasons above was a big deal to me too. but i feel like its important for me to also touch on the significance of WASD to me and why its here. a day before WASD happened i was informed by luna about WASD happening and i ended up tuning in for the celeste bingo race, since chris who took part in it also went to my sixth form. i donated a bit of money, and he made a funny face and it got turned into a sticker in the uwcs discord, i think that's awesome but more importantly, speedrunning has been a pretty big part of my life from when i was 11 up until about 16. i would spend a lot of time during year 7 learning the strats for things like super mario 63 and fancypants adventures, but i ended up getting quite well integrated into the roblox speedrunning community. i made myself quite known for my kind of timegroup and established a lot of people im still friends with today. i frequently modded the boards for pretty much all the tower obby games on speedrun.com (tower of hell, eternal towers of hell, anothers towers of stupidity, the neat project, to name a few) and it becamse a pretty important part of my early teenage experience (even though youre meant to be 17 to be allowed to do so, lol). but i was clocking in 3 hour shifts after school every single day to make sure the leaderboards were maintained, and eventually i became a verifier for the entire roblox category on speedrun.com which was a really big honour to me at the time, and i remember when i found out my application got accepted i did start crying on the spot. i ended up hosting for a few years this speedrun marathon called RGDQ (+ WRGDQ for during the winter) which was a biannual thing and me and the rest of the team ended up raising about $1500 on average per year. it was such an honour to be able to contribute to it. but in the process i gained a lot of skills, i did pretty much all the roles since it was a somewhat informal online thing, but as much as i was kinda bad at it, you cant expect much from someone whos like 13 trying to backend tech while also commentating and/or speedrunning. yeah, pretty big honour for me to have been a part of. but i stopped around the time of my a-levels, so being able to go to university and getting to experience a really well-done, official feeling speedrun marathon in person, and nevertheless getting to say i was an organiser for it brings me nearly to tears everytime i think about it. so nevertheless i want to say thank you to everyone who has contributed to wasd, everyone who has come to FNG and LAN, and especially, than you to all the execs who are passing the torch over to us and thank you Luna for introducing me to this amazing community, and im glad to see youre not leaving the exec team this year. <3
