# I'M TIRED BOSS 😩😩 | Newsletter T3W7


Captain’s Log, Exam Day No. 39:

Morale aboard the vessel is at an all-time low. The crew are bored. I am bored. We remain trapped in the cursed current of Eat, Sleep, Study, repeat, with no land in sight and burnout reaching levels previously thought impossible by modern science.

Supplies are dwindling. I have not touched grass in days. Human contact has been reduced to the occasional haunted glance exchanged with the library guards at 2am, both of us knowing I have long since abandoned hope.

In my weaker moments, I have entered into a polyamorous throuple with Instagram Reels and TikTok, each dragging me further from the path of academic salvation. At night, the dreams have begun: visions of slamming back £2 vodka Red Bulls in Smack, like some distant promised land whispered of by sailors gone mad.

Below deck, the WBS students in my flat have commenced their seventh consecutive night of festivities, partying until 3am while the rest of us ration sleep like ship’s biscuits. Welcome Week now feels like a tale from another lifetime, passed down by elders around the fire.

The horizon offers no answers. The sea is cruel. The exam timetable unrelenting.

When will this hell end?!?

On a more serious note: I hope everyone all doing well and hanging in there. As always, we have our usual schedule of events this week to help aid both your revision and socialising. If things are ever getting too much, below is a reminder that our welfare team are just a DM away ❤️. 

### 💜 Welfare Notice

UWCS has three Welfare Officers: Willow (Discord: `moshimoo`), Kevin (Discord: `yummycatfood`), and Erica (Discord: `heldesar`). They can also be reached via their email inbox at welfare@uwcs.co.uk.

You can speak to them at any time via Discord, email, and in person, for advice, signposting, and support. This can be for navigating the uni's support services, disclosing inappropriate or concerning behaviour at one of our events, and much more.

Any discussions you have with them will be treated sensitively. They will not share details more widely without your permission, except where they believe there is a serious risk of harm, a safeguarding concern, or another situation where they are required to escalate.
