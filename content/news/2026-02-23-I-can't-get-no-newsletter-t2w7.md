Hello everybody, newsletter's running a touch late this week - DCS's coursework load, as well as the internship grind, compressing our secretaries under the sort of pressure they make diamonds under. So, it's me again! I have no such limitations: I know I have work to do - I'm just not doing it. Today, we're gonna talk about a realisation I had this week.

Jealousy's a bitch, innit? You see, I am constantly surrounded at the moment with people interviewing for, or securing, internship offers at wonderful companies and other marvellous opportunities. Now that's all well and good and all, but in the process I've found myself a little melancholy. At this point, the people who know me in real life might strike me upside the head, knowing that I had these exact same things happening to me a few months ago; but the internall nagging doesn't cease. Could I have done better? Been more successful at this whole careers lark?

In the words of noted philosopher Mick Jagger, 'I can't get no satisfaction'. I've had this problem for years - from GCSE up until me writing this down. A lot of this has been academic (last year I had a genuine crash out over _getting a first_ because I still didn't think the mark was good enough), but there's been no small amount of social pressure ('Computer scientists have trouble with interpersonal relationships' - No Shit Magazine's Understatement of the Year). Upon discussing this with a friend, I was told: _'Don't make yourself unhappy overthinking things'_. This is the sentiment I'd like to leave you with today. Speaking of overthinking things, the intro is much longer than it has to be. Let's get a move on...
