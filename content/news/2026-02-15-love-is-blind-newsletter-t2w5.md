Hello everybody,

Our dearest secretaries are indisposed this weekend, and that means you're getting a slightly different vibe of newsletter this week. And coming off of Valentine's weekend, I thought we'd take a brief moment to talk about it.

Ah, love. Whether this weekend has been a whirlwind of affection - rapidly draining your bank balance into the red - or a solitary night in with naught but coursework and/or some videos of questionable repute to keep you company, love is a driving force we all need in our lives - whether we want to admit it or not. Some people claim that computer scientists, mathematicians, and those in adjacent fields are bad at managing interpersonal relationships. After 17 months as a university computer scientist - 15 of which have been spent observing our members as an exec of some capacity - these people are _100% indisputably correct_.

Nevertheless, if you have found yourself lonely, remember that you're worthwhile. There are _70 million_ people in this country, and _8 billion_ across the globe: statistically, there's someone out there for you. Part of the process of finding this person is going out and meeting as many people with common interests as possible. And do you know where you can find these people? **UWCS events!** Which reminds me, I should really start writing about those...

Quite a fun week this week, so keep an eye out for announcements in the days to come. Also (if I get this out on time), our 10th annual **WASD** is still going on, and has already smashed its goals and raised over **£2,800** so far. Every penny goes to **SpecialEffect**, a charity that brings the fun of gaming to physically disabled people and children you'd otherwise miss out. It's a fantastic cause, so if you have any money left over this weekend, head over to [warwickspeed.run](https://warwickspeed.run/) and donate :)

Now, onwards!
